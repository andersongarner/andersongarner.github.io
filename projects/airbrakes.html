UVA Rocketry – Airbrakes Sub-team Lead

August 2024 – Present

Project Overview

Led the design and development of an active airbrakes system for UVA Rocketry’s 2025 IREC entry, contributing to the team’s 1st Place finish in the 10k COTS category.

Currently guiding the next-generation system with enhanced design precision and manufacturability.

  • Role: Sub-team Lead (managing 29 members)
  • Tools: SolidWorks, ANSYS Fluent, CNC machining
  • Key Outcome: 1st Place, IREC 10k COTS Category (2025)

Challenge

The rocket required an active drag-control system to reliably target a 10,000 ft apogee. The mechanism needed to be lightweight, structurally sound, and manufacturable under strict deadlines.

Approach

I directed the mechanical design effort, creating detailed SolidWorks models and performing FEA simulations to validate structural performance. To meet deployment requirements, I developed a custom curved cam surface that ensured a linear relationship between actuator rotation and fin extension.

Results

Due to environmental changes at the competition site (lower elevation and higher air density) and increased rocket mass, the system was not deployed to full capability. Nevertheless, the design’s rigor and documentation significantly boosted our technical evaluation scores, securing a 1st Place finish in the IREC 10k COTS category.

Reflection

This project reinforced the value of precise technical communication across sub-teams and highlighted the trade-offs between simulation fidelity and manufacturing feasibility.
